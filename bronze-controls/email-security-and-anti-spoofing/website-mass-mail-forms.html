At BCSF we often speak in terms of policy statements that originate from management and then are applied through practices and procedures; most importantly, they are there to fit your organizational business practices.  When it comes to securing third-party e-mail forms and systems the implementation of BCSF's policy statement is not optional because many systems have already made systemic changes to force compliance.  However, other systems - like your organization's website - may not have adapted and thus it is critical to understand and act as your organization be open to abuse or miss important e-mail communications.

Support Systems and Automatic Replies

Your support systems are also affected by e-mail protections.  For example, if you use ZenDesk, you may have already realized that they implemented a programming change that prevented certain variables from working on the very first e-mail to avoid abuse. This affected all new tickets as follows:

  • When a new ticket is created, it is not possible to include the body of the request in the confirmation message
  • When a new ticket is created, the subject of the message is not honored and the only option is to send a generic subjects such as “Request Received.”
